# DisasterTrust AI Demo Video Script

Target length: 90-120 seconds
Focus: citizen report -> volunteer response -> live rescue tracking -> admin visibility

## 1. Opening Hook (0:00-0:10)
On screen:
- Show the landing page hero and the trust-score preview.
- Pause on the line about verified confidence scoring.

Narration:
"During disasters, people, volunteers, NGOs, and authorities all need the same thing: trustworthy information, fast. DisasterTrust AI is a community-powered disaster response platform built to solve that problem."

## 2. Problem Statement (0:10-0:20)
On screen:
- Briefly show the landing page or a text slide.
- Highlight the idea that information is scattered across WhatsApp, Telegram, phone calls, and social media.

Narration:
"In real emergencies, reports are scattered and often unreliable. Fake or outdated information wastes precious time. DisasterTrust AI adds trust scoring and live evidence to every report."

## 3. Citizen Reporting Flow (0:20-0:45)
On screen:
- Open /report.
- Show the role banner.
- Select Flood.
- Continue to live evidence.
- Start and stop the camera recording step.
- Continue to the description step.
- Enter a short emergency description.
- Continue to review.

Narration:
"A citizen opens the app and reports an emergency. The flow requires live camera evidence, automatic GPS, and a locked timestamp so old uploads cannot be passed off as current events."

Callout text to show:
- Live video required
- GPS locked
- Timestamp locked
- AI confidence score

## 4. Submit + Trust Score (0:45-0:55)
On screen:
- Submit the report.
- Show the success screen.
- Highlight the confidence score.
- Highlight the live rescue tracker card.

Narration:
"Once submitted, the report is classified by AI and assigned a trust score. The user immediately sees a live rescue tracker, similar to how a delivery app shows a driver approaching."

## 5. Volunteer Response (0:55-1:15)
On screen:
- Switch to /volunteer.
- Show the new incident at the top of the queue.
- Click Accept Request.
- Click Mark En Route.
- Optionally click Confirm Report.

Narration:
"Volunteers see verified, high-priority reports first. They can accept the request, update the status, and confirm the report so the confidence grows as the rescue progresses."

Callout text to show:
- Highest confidence first
- Accept Request
- Mark En Route
- Mark Complete

## 6. Live Tracker Update (1:15-1:30)
On screen:
- Return to the citizen success screen.
- Show the live rescue tracker updating from waiting to on the way.
- If possible, show the progress moving along the route and the ETA changing.

Narration:
"The citizen sees the rescue status update live. As the volunteer accepts and travels, the tracker advances in real time so the person in distress knows help is coming."

## 7. Admin Visibility (1:30-1:45)
On screen:
- Open /admin.
- Show the live map, active incidents, and critical alerts.

Narration:
"Authorities and admins get situational awareness in one place. They can monitor active incidents, live map locations, and the overall status of rescue operations."

## 8. Closing (1:45-1:55)
On screen:
- Return to the landing page or end on the logo.

Narration:
"DisasterTrust AI turns emergency reports into trusted, trackable rescue operations, so response teams can act faster and communities can see help moving toward them in real time."

## Recording Tips
- Keep the demo moving; avoid long pauses.
- Use a clean browser window with no extra tabs.
- If the camera flow is slow, pre-fill the description step so the recording feels smooth.
- Highlight the tracker and volunteer status changes with a cursor pause or zoom if your recorder supports it.

## Short One-Line Pitch
"DisasterTrust AI is a disaster response platform that verifies emergency reports, prioritizes urgent cases, and shows citizens a live rescue tracker while volunteers are on the way."
